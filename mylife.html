My Early Life:

I grew up in Northern California, in a city called Palo Alto. We lived just outside of San Fransico and down the road from Stanford University. It was a beautiful and diverse place, with great weather all year round.

When I was 14 years old, we moved to Highland, Utah to be closer to family. While I have come to love Utah and people who live here, I initially found it difficult to adjust to this new culture. It was quite different from the diversity and openness I had become accustomed to in California. While this move proved challenging, it also brought welcome opportunities for growth that enabled me to deepen my empathy and understanding for others. I still find myself aware of individuals who seem to be lonley or those who may feel out of place and try to help them in any way I can.
